How it works

Three steps. No fluff.

Disconnector doesn't delete apps, doesn't lock you out of your phone, and doesn't ship your data anywhere. Here's the entire mechanism.

01

Pick the apps

Open the Apps tab. Every app installed on your phone shows up — social, browsers, work, games. Tap the ones you want quieted. Change the list any time.

02

Choose how to start

Tap Disconnect to cut those apps off until you reconnect, or pick a duration: 15, 30, 60, 120 minutes. Or set a recurring schedule that fires automatically.

03

The apps go quiet

Selected apps lose their internet. They open normally — feeds just don't load, messages don't send, videos don't stream. Everything else on your phone is untouched.

The whole mechanism

It's a local switch. Not a VPN tunnel.

Android calls it a VPN because that's the system API we use. But nothing connects out, nothing routes through a server, and nothing leaves your phone. Think of it as a per-app light switch — we turn off the light only for the apps you choose.

Questions

Frequently asked.

Does this delete my apps?
No. Your apps stay exactly where they are. Your logins, your saved data, your notifications history — all untouched. Disconnector only cuts the apps' internet. The moment you reconnect, they work normally again.
Is this a real VPN? Will you see my traffic?
No. Disconnector uses Android's VpnService API because that's the only way to do per-app network control on Android — but it's a local-only VPN. There's no upstream tunnel. Packets from the apps you've selected are read and discarded right on your phone. Nothing routes through any server, and we have no infrastructure that could read your traffic even if we wanted to.
Can I block browsers entirely?
Yes. Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Samsung Internet, Brave, DuckDuckGo, Opera, Vivaldi — they all live in the Browsers category. Add them to your quiet list like any other app. This is something you can't do on iOS, where Safari is exempt from per-app VPN controls.
Can I add apps that aren't in the catalog?
There's no catalog to add to. Disconnector reads the actual list of apps installed on your phone — every launchable app you have shows up in the Apps tab, sorted into Social, Streaming, Browsers, Games, Work, and other categories.
Why didn't my schedule fire?
Schedules use Android's exact-alarm system, which fires reliably even when the phone is idle. If a schedule misses, check Android Settings → Apps → Disconnector → Alarms & reminders. That permission must be on. On a few aggressive battery-saver OEM forks (Xiaomi, OnePlus older devices), you may also need to disable battery optimization for Disconnector.
What happens if I open a quieted app?
It opens normally and shows whatever it last had cached. Try to refresh and you'll see a connection error. Some apps have offline modes that still work — that's fine. The point isn't to freeze the app; it's to cut the refresh-refresh-refresh loop.
Does this drain the battery?
Very little. We run a lightweight VPN service that drops packets instead of forwarding them — no encryption, no uplink, no heavy work. In testing, we see drain similar to having any always-on VPN app installed.
Why is the timer counting up instead of down?
You started an indefinite session — no end time, runs until you tap Reconnect. Timed sessions (the 15m / 30m / 1h / 2h chips) count down.
What does Lock actually protect?
When Lock is on, you can't reconnect an active session or change your quiet list without entering your passcode (or biometric, if enabled). Useful for committing to a quiet window you couldn't otherwise resist breaking.
Can I run another VPN at the same time?
No. Android only allows one VPN service at a time. While Disconnector's local VPN is up, you can't run another VPN app. This is an Android platform constraint, not a Disconnector limitation.
